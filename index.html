We believe that every training, every supportive word and every community experience can be a turning point in a young person’s life.

Our mission

The Anti-Drug World Federation is an umbrella organization bringing together sports clubs, associations and civil initiatives that use sport as a tool for drug prevention, motivation and community building.

Our goal is to offer real alternatives to young people: meaningful activities, supportive communities and strong role models instead of destructive substances.

We work to ensure that every country and every city has sports clubs that young people can turn to when they want change, support or a new start.

Key focus areas

• Youth drug prevention through sport

• Support for vulnerable and at-risk groups

• Sport-based social reintegration

• International cooperation between clubs and NGOs

• Awareness campaigns and educational programs

Programs and international network

We build an international network of sports clubs and NGOs that accept young people into training if they show commitment to change and a drug-free life.

Our long-term vision is that in every country there will be partner clubs where young people can join training sessions simply by presenting a supporting token or reference – as a sign of their decision to step away from drugs and towards sport.

The Federation coordinates joint campaigns, training programs, seminars and awareness events, and supports national partners in developing their own local prevention projects.

Leadership

Attila Tóth
President

Attila Tóth is a committed advocate of sport-based drug prevention and community development. He has decades of experience supporting and motivating young people, helping them grow through the transformative power of sport.

His work is defined by strong social responsibility, dedication to youth protection and a clear mission to strengthen communities. Deputy President

Secretary General

Gyula Gál is a key figure of the organization, known for initiating several reintegration-focused sport programs to support successful returns to society.

He has extensive experience in sports education, social development and rehabilitation. As Secretary General, he is responsible for organizational management, international documentation and the professional coordination of development projects.
